Who We Are

A student movement for every teen who feels unheard.

TeenSpace India was born from a simple truth: too many teenagers in India are struggling in silence. We are a student-led organization that believes peer voices hold power. We use storytelling, content, creative expression, and community to create safe mental health conversations — everywhere from school hallways to Instagram feeds.

Our Mission

What we stand for — and why it matters.

TeenSpace is a global movement — open to every teen, everywhere. Anyone across the world can start their own TeenSpace chapter and carry this mission into their own community. Every chapter is independent, locally led, and powered entirely by the passion of young people who believe change starts with them.

Through honest storytelling, advocacy, and community-driven initiatives, every chapter works to break the stigma around mental health and foster understanding, connection, and hope in their corner of the world.

The India chapter exists because our Chapter Lead, Preety Shahare, felt deeply called to bring this movement home. She wanted to work for the millions of Indian teens who struggle in silence — and so she did. TeenSpace India was born because one student chose to act.

The Reality

Mental health issues facing Indian teens today.

India has over 436 million children and adolescents. Mental health services have lagged significantly, leaving a massive treatment gap compounded by stigma, lack of awareness, and limited resources in schools.

😔

Depression

Depression is the most prevalent mental health issue among school-age adolescents in India, often going unrecognised until it becomes severe dysfunction.

Most Common
😰

Anxiety & Stress

Academic pressure, competitive exams, and social media overexposure have pushed 40% of Indian teenagers to cite stress and anxiety as their primary concern.

40% of Teens
📱

Social Media & Isolation

AI-driven social platforms exploit psychological vulnerabilities, fuelling unhealthy comparisons, cyberbullying, and social isolation among teens.

Rising Fast
🧠

Emotional & Behavioural Issues

Studies show 14% of students experience extreme emotional episodes and 43% deal with mood swings — symptoms that often precede more serious conditions.

14–43% Affected
🏫

No Support in Schools

Most schools lack trained counsellors. Mental health is absent from the standard curriculum, leaving students without any professional support system.

Systemic Gap
🚨

Suicide Risk

Suicide has become the leading cause of death among Indian youth. A 7.3% prevalence of depression and anxiety among adolescents is driving this crisis.

Critical Alert
By The Numbers

The issues that matter most in India's teen mental health crisis.

🔍

Stigma & Silence

Mental health remains deeply stigmatised in Indian homes and classrooms, with families often missing warning signs until a crisis occurs.

📚

Academic Pressure

India's hyper-competitive academic environment — board exams, entrance tests, ranking anxiety — is a major driver of student stress and burnout.

👥

Peer Pressure & Bullying

Social conformity pressures and cyberbullying via social platforms disproportionately affect teenagers, often triggering anxiety and social phobia.

🏠

Family Expectations

Cultural and economic factors around career choices and family honour create significant emotional burdens for teenagers that rarely get acknowledged.

🌐

Urban-Rural Divide

Mental health resources are concentrated in cities. Rural and semi-urban students face even greater gaps in access to support, counselling, and awareness.
